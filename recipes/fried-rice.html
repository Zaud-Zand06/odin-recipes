Fried Rice

Description

I love fried rice so much. I could write a manifesto about it here but I'll save us some time and instead blow your fucking socks off with this incoming fried rice recipe. This shit will make you punch your grandmother in her big toe. This recipe will make you slap your dad on the hairline. Get ready this one is a kicker.

Also don't bother asking for measurements on any of this, I cook this with my heart so I can't tell you anything about how much of each ingredient I use. I just know what my tastes are and adjust for myself.

Ingredients

  • Rice
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Spam
  • Eggs
  • Soy Sauce
  • Salt
  • Powdered Chicken Stock
  • MSG
  • Peas

The Steps:

  1. Prepare the Ingredients

    2. This first step of preperation is arguably the most important. Make sure you have day-old rice for this one. The dryness of the rice helps the individual grains stay more crispy and give it a better body when you bite into it. If you like mush, freshly cooked is fine.

    Finely mince the ginger and the garlic. Cut the spam into either 1 cm cubes or small strips. The way you cut the spam doesn't really matter here, as long as it is easy to eat.

  2. Frying

    3. This is the hardest part of the recipe.

    Bring your pan up to a medium high and let some vegetable oil heat up in it.

    As the oil is heating, add the spam into the pot and gently mix so the spam is all seperated and coated in oil. In a few minutes the spam will begin to brown and develop a nice colour, at this point you can add the minced garlic and ginger. Sautee all these together for a couple minutes before cracking in a few eggs. Break the egg yolks and let the eggs cook for a bit.

    Generally I add an egg per person eating, or an egg per serving.

    Once the eggs are slightly cooked you can add the rice to the pan. Break up any clumps of rice that you can see and gently stir it all together. At this point you should also crank you stove to high.

    Keep stirring and breaking up the rice as you add some soy sauce, salt, chicken powder and msg to the rice. Be moderate with the msg, a little goes a long way in this dish. At this point you can add whatever additional spices you may want; white pepper, bbq rub, ramen powder, etc.

    Once the rice has been seasoned add the peas and turn off the heat. Keep stirring the peas into the rice but the residual heat should be all the peas need to cook without losing their vibrancy. Now add a drizzle of seasame oil and give it one last stir before serving.
